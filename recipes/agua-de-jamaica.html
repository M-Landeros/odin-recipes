Agua de Jamaica (Hibiscus Tea)

Description

Agua de Jamaica or Hibiscus tea is a drink that is part of a group of drinks known as agua frescas. Made with water infused with dried flor de Jamaica or Hibiscus flowers. Commonly found in Africa, India, Jamaica, and Mexico, this sweet-tart drink has a similar flavor to cranberry. An icy glass Agua de Jamaica, paired with a lime wedge and sweetened with sugar or honey, is the perfect drink on a hot summer day.

Ingredients:

  • 12 cups water
  • 1.5 cups dried Hibiscus flower (about 2.5 ounces)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • Ice and lime wedges, for serving (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine 4 cups water and 1/5 cups dried Hibiscus flowers. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer until the dried flowers have softened, about 15 minutes.
  2. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve to remove flowers, pressing down to extract as much liquid as possible (should get approximately 3 cups).
  3. Stir in sugar until dissolved. Transfer to a large pitcher and sti in remaining 8 cups cold water. Serve over ice with lime wedges if desired.
