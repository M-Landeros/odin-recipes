Champurrado

Description

Champurrado is a thick, warm, and sweet Mexican chocolate drink that's a type of atole, a sweet, warm Mexican drink made with a masa harina base. It's often made with Mexican chocolate, piloncillo (Mexican brown sugar), and masa de maiz, masa harina, or corn flour. Other ingredients that can be added include cinnamon, anise seed, vanilla, ground nuts, orange zest, and egg. Champurrado has a richer, grainier consistency than atole, which is made with cornstarch for a smoother, creamier texture

Ingredients:

  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 whole star anise
  • 1/4 cup masa harina
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 oz finely chopped piloncillo
  • 1/2 tablet of Ibarra or Abuelita chocolate

Directions:

  1. Bring 3 cups of water to a boil and add the cinnamon and star anise. Remove from heat and let water steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Remove the cinnamon and star anise from the pot and return to low heat
  3. Slowly add the masa, until completely dissolved
  4. Add piloncillo, chocolate, and milk. Mix until fully dissolved
  5. Use a whisk or molinillo whisk to froth the drink
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
